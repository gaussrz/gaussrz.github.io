PDE

Transport equations is a class of PDEs that is usually used to describe the 'density' of some physical quantities. Heat equation, air dynamical equation and many others are special cases of transport equations. Transport equation determines the dynamical behavior of the quantity.

the Law of Balance

The key idea of transport equation is 'the law of balance', that is, the transport equation is always derived from the balance of the physical quantity in an arbitrary domain of space-time. Suppose we want to describe dynamics of some physical quantity \(Q\) in a domain \(\Omega\subset\mathbb R^n\) that evolutes from time \(0\) to \(\infty\) with density \(u(t, x)\), flux \(\vec F(t, x)\), and source (the quantity appears/disappears from/to 'nowhere') \(f(t, x)\).
